Texas Hold 'em Poker

Complete Guide & Rules

Basic Gameplay

  1. Each player is dealt 2 cards face down to start the game.
  2. The player to the left of the dealer must bet half of the minimum bet (small blind).
  3. The next player must bet the minimum bet (big blind).
  4. Players can then choose to call (match the bet), raise (bet more), or fold (quit the round).
  5. After the first betting round, three community cards are dealt face up (the flop).
  6. Another round of betting occurs, then a fourth card is dealt (the turn).
  7. More betting, then the fifth and final card is dealt (the river).
  8. Final betting round, then remaining players show their cards to determine the winner.

Poker Hand Rankings

Only the best 5 cards count, even though 7 cards are available. Hands are ranked from strongest to weakest:

Strategy Tips

Starting Hands

Only play rounds with strong starting cards like A-K, A-Q, K-K, Q-Q, or J-J. Fold weak hands like 7-2 or mismatched low cards.

Read the Board

Always consider what hands your opponents might have. Watch for possible straights, flushes, or sets before betting big.

Bankroll Management

Don't bet more than you can afford to lose. Conservative betting with occasional aggressive plays often wins more than constant big bets.

Bluffing

Bluff sparingly and with purpose. A well-timed bluff can win big pots, but overuse makes you predictable.
